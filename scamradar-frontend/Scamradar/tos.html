1. Nature of the Platform

ScamRadar is a community-driven, anonymous scam awareness platform that allows members of the public to share their experiences with suspected fraudulent phone numbers, UPI IDs, and email addresses. The Platform is designed to help individuals make more informed decisions before engaging with unknown parties.

ScamRadar is not a law enforcement agency, regulatory body, or judicial authority. We do not investigate, verify, adjudicate, or draw legal conclusions about any person, entity, or reported incident. Nothing on this Platform constitutes a finding of guilt, criminal record, legal judgment, or official designation of any kind.

Important: All content on this Platform represents the personal experiences and opinions of individual community members. ScamRadar makes no representation as to the truth, accuracy, completeness, or reliability of any report, and expressly disclaims any such representation.

2. Intermediary Status & IT Act Section 79

ScamRadar operates as an intermediary as defined under Section 2(1)(w) of the Information Technology Act, 2000 ("IT Act") and is entitled to the safe harbour protections under Section 79 of the IT Act, subject to compliance with the applicable rules and guidelines.

As an intermediary, ScamRadar:

  • Does not initiate the transmission of user-submitted reports
  • Does not select the receivers of user-submitted content
  • Does not modify the information contained in user-submitted reports
  • Acts as a passive conduit for user-generated content

ScamRadar complies with the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021 and maintains a designated Grievance Officer as required.

ScamRadar's intermediary status means that legal liability for the content of reports rests with the user who submitted the report, not with ScamRadar, provided ScamRadar acts expeditiously upon receiving valid takedown requests.

Users viewing this Platform must exercise their own independent judgment and are strongly advised not to make critical financial or safety decisions based solely on information found here.

7. Report Threshold & Display Policy

To reduce the risk of false positives and protect individuals from isolated malicious reports, ScamRadar applies the following display policy:

  • An entity will not be publicly highlighted until it has received reports from a minimum of 3 independent users
  • Single-report entities are displayed only with a prominent low-confidence disclaimer
  • All results — regardless of report count — carry a clear disclaimer that reports are unverified community submissions

This threshold policy does not guarantee that all displayed reports are accurate, but it significantly reduces the likelihood of a single malicious actor weaponising the Platform against an innocent party.

8. Score Decay & Accuracy

ScamRadar applies an automatic activity decay system to all flagged entities. The confidence indicators associated with an entity are automatically reduced based on the time elapsed since the last report was submitted:

  • Under 30 days — Full weight
  • 30–90 days — 10% reduction
  • 90–180 days — 25% reduction
  • 180–365 days — 50% reduction
  • Over 365 days — 75% reduction

This system ensures that old, potentially stale reports do not permanently and unfairly stigmatise an entity, particularly in cases where phone numbers change ownership or situations are resolved.

11. Cyber Crime Reporting

ScamRadar strongly encourages all users who have been victims of online fraud to also file an official complaint with Indian law enforcement authorities:

  • National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
  • National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24/7, toll-free)
  • Local police: File an FIR at your nearest police station

ScamRadar is a community awareness tool and does not replace official law enforcement channels. Only official authorities have the power to investigate, arrest, and prosecute cyber criminals and to facilitate the recovery of stolen funds.

Reports submitted on ScamRadar that include an official Cyber Crime complaint acknowledgement number receive a "Police Complaint Filed ✓" indicator, which increases the credibility weight of the report in our community system.
